Numbers that we see in our everyday life can be classified into multiple types.


Some of the number types are Whole Numbers, Natural Numbers, Integers, Rational Numbers, Irrational Numbers, and Real Numbers


Whole Numbers: A number that is not negative and does not have fractional part. Examples : 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, ...


Natural Numbers: Counting numbers are called natural numbers. It starts from 1 and above. Examples : 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, ...



Integers: All whole numbers are including positive and negative numbers are Integers. Examples : ... , -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, ...

Rational Numbers: Numbers that can be expressed by fraction or ratio. It comprises of Natural Numbers, Whole Numbers, Integers together. Examples : 3/4 also written as 0.75, 0.50 or 1/2, 1/3 which can be written as 0.⁻3

Irrational Numbers: Numbers that cannot be converted to fractions because the decimal keeps going without repeating. Examples : 3.14159265..., sqrt(2)

Real Numbers: All numbers that can be plotted on number line. It is a superset of Natural Numbers, Whole Numbers, Integers, Rational Numbers, and Irrational Numbers. (All of the above) Examples : 2, -11, 1.782, pi, 3.4, √6, etc.
